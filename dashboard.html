Key Insights

  • Across 91 sampled issues, the median cycle time (In Progress → Done) is 5.0 days, with a mean of 4.91 days. The 85th percentile is 8.17 days and 95th percentile is 9.0 days.
  • Cycle times range from 0.01 to 9.93 days (spread of 9.9 days), indicating significant variability. High variability reduces predictability of delivery commitments.
  • 5 issues spent more than half a day blocked. Among those, the average blocked time was 3.8 days. Reducing blocked time is one of the highest-leverage improvements.
  • 3 issue(s) went directly from Backlog to Done without entering In Progress (BIP-26741, BIP-28166, BIP-28438). These were excluded from cycle time calculations.
  • Cycle times increased over time: the first 4 sprints averaged 4.6-day median vs 5.0 days in the last 4 (~9% increase). Investigate growing complexity or WIP limits.
  • Average throughput: first 4 sprints = 67.0 issues/sprint, last 4 sprints = 62.0 issues/sprint.
  • Flow efficiency (active work / lead time): median 78%. Lead time includes backlog wait before work starts. Higher efficiency means less waiting. World-class teams target >40%.
  • Median lead time (9.19d) exceeds median cycle time (5.0d) by 4.2 days, meaning issues sit in Backlog for a median of ~4.2 days before work begins.
